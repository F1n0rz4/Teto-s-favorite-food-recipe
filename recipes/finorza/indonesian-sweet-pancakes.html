Indonesian Sweet Pancakes (Kue Bandung)

Description

Indonesian Sweet Pancakes (often known as Kue Bandung or Martabak Manis) are one of Indonesia’s most iconic dessert treats. Soft, fluffy, and delightfully rich, this sweet delicacy is a favorite among dessert lovers across the country. Traditionally cooked on a thick pan until perfectly golden, the pancake is folded in half and generously filled with luscious ingredients like chocolate, crushed peanuts, cheese, condensed milk, and sometimes even sesame seeds or butter for extra richness.

For many, including myself, Kue Bandung is the go-to treat whenever a sweet craving strikes. The peanut-chocolate variant remains a timeless favorite. Its warm, nutty crunch combined with melty chocolate creates a heavenly flavor and texture that’s impossible to resist.

What makes Kue Bandung truly special is its combination of simplicity and indulgence. The inside is soft and airy, the edges are slightly crisp, and the filling melts into every layer, offering a comforting sweetness that feels nostalgic and satisfying with every bite. Whether enjoyed as a late-night snack, an afternoon treat, or during special family gatherings, this dessert never fails to bring joy.

If you’ve never tried Kue Bandung before, it’s a dessert you absolutely shouldn’t miss. Its unique texture, rich flavors, and warm, freshly-cooked aroma make it an unforgettable culinary experience. One bite is all it takes to understand why Kue Bandung has remained one of Indonesia’s most beloved sweet creations for generations.

Ingredients

And the good news is, you can make it on home, and the ingredients are simple to get, here it is:

  • A. Base Batter

    • 225 g medium-protein wheat flour

    • 20 g cornstarch

    • 30 g granulated sugar

    • 250 ml water

  • B. Egg Mixture

    • 1 egg

    • ¾ tsp baking powder

  • C. Pandan Batter Add-In

    • 25 ml water

    • 1.5 g baking soda

    • 1 tsp pandan paste

  • D. Original Batter Add-In

    • 25 ml water

    • 1.5 g baking soda

    • 1 tsp vanilla extract

  • E. Additional Ingredients

    • Granulated sugar, as needed (for sprinkling or breaking surface bubbles during cooking)

    • Butter, for greasing (or margarine for an economical option)

  • F. Fillings (Adjust to Taste)

    • Sweetened condensed milk

    • Grated cheddar cheese

    • Chocolate sprinkles

    • Crushed peanuts

    • Sesame seeds

Instructions

Here's the instructions that we can follow along:

  1. Prepare The Base Batter

    1. In a mixing bowl, combine all Ingredients A. Whisk using a hand whisk or a mixer until just smooth.

    2. Add Ingredients B (egg and baking powder). Mix briefly until fully incorporated.

    3. Cover the batter with a clean cloth and let it rest for at least 1 hour.

  2. Divide and Flavor the Batter

    1. After resting, the batter is ready to use. Prepare Ingredients C (Pandan Add-In) and Ingredients D (Original Add-In).

    2. Divide the batter into two equal portions:

      • One for Pandan Martabak

      • One for Original Martabak

    3. Add Ingredient C to the pandan portion and Ingredient D to the original portion. Fold gently with a spatula until just combined(do not overmix.)

  3. Cooking the Martabak (Same Method for Both Variants)

    1. Heat a pan over medium heat. Ensure it is hot but not smoking.

    2. Pour in one portion of the batter. Swirl the pan slightly to form a thin crust around the edges.

    3. Cook without moving the pan until bubbles appear on the surface.

    4. Sprinkle granulated sugar over the bubbling areas to help the bubbles open.

    5. Cover the pan and continue cooking over low heat for 3–5 minutes, or until the surface is fully set and no longer wet.

    6. Remove the cooked martabak onto a cutting board. The bottom should be lightly browned, not burnt.

  4. Finishing and Filling

    • A. Pandan Martabak

      1. While still hot, brush the surface generously with butter (or margarine).

      2. Add fillings as desired, Such as:

        • Sweetened condensed milk

        • Grated cheddar cheese

        • Chocolate sprinkles

        • Crushed peanuts

        • Sesame seeds

      3. Cut the martabak in half, fold it over, brush the top with butter or margarine, and slice as desired.

    • B. Original Martabak

      1. While still hot, brush the surface generously with butter (or margarine).

      2. Add fillings as desired, such as:

        • Sweetened condensed milk

        • Grated cheddar cheese

        • Chocolate sprinkles

        • Crushed peanuts

        • Sesame seeds

      3. Cut the martabak in half, fold it, brush the top with butter or margarine, and slice to your preferred size.
